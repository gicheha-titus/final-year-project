# Appendix: Semi-Structured Interview Guide

1. How is pathway guidance currently handled for CBC learners in your school?
2. What information do teachers and parents usually rely on when discussing readiness for a pathway or track?
3. What challenges arise when trying to interpret learner performance across multiple terms and subjects?
4. If a system predicts that a learner appears more suited to a certain pathway, what explanation would you need before trusting that output?
5. Which subjects or learning patterns do you consider most important when judging readiness for STEM, Social Sciences, or Arts and Sports Science?
6. How should the system present uncertainty so that staff do not treat the output as a fixed placement decision?
7. What type of printable report would be most useful during an academic clinic or parent meeting?
8. What constraints in your school environment should the system accommodate? For example: computer access, internet reliability, staff digital literacy, or time pressure.
9. What ethical or fairness concerns would you want addressed before using an AI-assisted guidance tool?
10. What would make this system practically useful rather than just technically interesting?
